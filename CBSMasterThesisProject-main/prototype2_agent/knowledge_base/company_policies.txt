AdventureWorks Corporate Policies & Compliance Guidelines
==========================================================

This document defines all corporate policies, compliance requirements, data governance
rules, and operational standards for AdventureWorks Cycles. These policies apply to all
automated reports, dashboards, ad-hoc analyses, and data exports produced by the BI system.
Violation of these policies may result in audit findings or regulatory penalties.


================================================================================
1. DATA ACCESS & PRIVACY (PII PROTECTION)
================================================================================

1.1 Personally Identifiable Information (PII) Classification
  The following database fields contain PII and are subject to access restrictions:

  HIGH SENSITIVITY (never expose in reports):
  - person.password: passwordhash, passwordsalt
  - humanresources.employee: nationalidnumber

  MEDIUM SENSITIVITY (aggregate only, minimum group size 5):
  - person.person: firstname, middlename, lastname, suffix, title
  - person.emailaddress: emailaddress
  - person.personphone: phonenumber
  - person.address: addressline1, addressline2, city, postalcode
  - humanresources.employee: loginid, birthdate, gender, maritalstatus

  LOW SENSITIVITY (permitted in internal reports with justification):
  - person.person: persontype, emailpromotion
  - person.businessentity: businessentityid (identifier only, no PII by itself)
  - humanresources.employee: jobtitle, hiredate, currentflag

1.2 PII Access Rules
  - NEVER return passwordhash or passwordsalt in any query result under any circumstances.
  - NEVER return nationalidnumber in any report or export.
  - Individual names (firstname, lastname) may appear in internal operational reports
    (e.g., salesperson performance) but must be removed from any externally shared report.
  - Email addresses and phone numbers require business justification and must not be
    included in bulk exports exceeding 100 records without data governance approval.
  - Employee home addresses are NEVER included in reports. Person addresses may only appear
    in customer-facing contexts (shipping, billing) and internal logistics reports.

1.3 Minimum Aggregation Group Size
  Any report that breaks down data by a demographic dimension (gender, marital status,
  birth year, etc.) must ensure each group contains at least 5 individuals.
  If a group has fewer than 5 members, it must be combined with adjacent groups or
  suppressed and labeled "Other" to prevent re-identification.

1.4 Data Masking Requirements
  When PII must appear in development or testing environments:
  - Email addresses: mask as f***@domain.com
  - Phone numbers: show only last 4 digits
  - Names: use initials only (e.g., J.S.)
  - Addresses: show only city and state, redact street addresses

1.5 Cross-Reference Restrictions
  Queries must not join person.password with any other table in a result set.
  Salary data (humanresources.employeepayhistory.rate) must never be joined with
  person.person name fields in the same output — aggregate by department or job title instead.

2. FINANCIAL REPORTING STANDARDS
================================================================================

2.1 Revenue Definition (Canonical)
  Revenue is ALWAYS calculated as:
    unitprice * orderqty * (1 - unitpricediscount)
  from sales.salesorderdetail.

  The following are NOT revenue:
  - salesorderheader.subtotal: this is a header-level pre-calculated field and may
    differ slightly due to rounding. Use line-level calculation for precision.
  - salesorderheader.totaldue: this INCLUDES tax and freight. Only use when the
    report explicitly asks for "total due" or "gross order value."
  - salesorderheader.taxamt: report separately as "Tax Collected."
  - salesorderheader.freight: report separately as "Freight/Shipping Cost."

2.2 Tax and Freight Separation
  Tax (taxamt) and freight MUST be reported as separate line items in financial reports.
  They are NEVER included in "revenue" or "sales" figures unless the metric is
  explicitly labeled "Total Due" or "Gross Order Value."
  For internal management reports: Revenue + Tax + Freight = Total Due.

2.3 Fiscal Year Calendar
  AdventureWorks operates on a July-June fiscal year:
  - Fiscal Year Start: July 1
  - Fiscal Year End: June 30
  - Q1: July 1 - September 30
  - Q2: October 1 - December 31
  - Q3: January 1 - March 31
  - Q4: April 1 - June 30

  Fiscal year number: If the calendar month is >= July, the fiscal year is
  calendar_year + 1. Example: October 2013 is FY2014 Q2.

  SQL: CASE WHEN EXTRACT(MONTH FROM date) >= 7
         THEN EXTRACT(YEAR FROM date) + 1
         ELSE EXTRACT(YEAR FROM date) END AS fiscal_year

2.4 Currency Handling
  - Base reporting currency: USD (United States Dollar).
  - Orders with salesorderheader.currencyrateid IS NULL are in USD.
  - Orders with currencyrateid populated must be converted using sales.currencyrate.
  - For multi-currency reports, always show both local currency amount and USD equivalent.
  - Exchange rate: use endofdayrate from sales.currencyrate for closing positions,
    averagerate for period averages.
  - Currency codes are in sales.currency (3-character ISO codes).

2.5 Rounding Rules
  - All monetary values: ROUND to 2 decimal places in final output.
  - Percentages: ROUND to 1 decimal place (e.g., 45.3%).
  - Quantities: always integers, no rounding needed.
  - Rates (pay rate, tax rate): ROUND to 4 decimal places for accuracy.

2.6 Period Comparisons
  When computing period-over-period metrics (MoM, QoQ, YoY):
  - NEVER use CURRENT_DATE or NOW() — the data is historical (2011-2014).
  - Always anchor to MAX(orderdate) from sales.salesorderheader.
  - Use DATE_TRUNC for clean period boundaries.
  - Always include both lower and upper bounds in WHERE clauses.
  - INTERVAL syntax: always INTERVAL '12 months' (not INTERVAL '12' MONTHS).

2.7 Order Status Definitions
  Sales orders (sales.salesorderheader.status):
  - 1 = In Process
  - 2 = Approved
  - 3 = Backordered
  - 4 = Rejected
  - 5 = Shipped
  - 6 = Cancelled
  Revenue reports should only include status = 5 (Shipped) unless explicitly asked
  for pipeline or backlog analysis.

  Purchase orders (purchasing.purchaseorderheader.status):
  - 1 = Pending
  - 2 = Approved
  - 3 = Rejected
  - 4 = Complete
  Spend reports should only include status = 4 (Complete) for confirmed expenditures.

2.8 Credit Card Data Handling
  - sales.creditcard.cardnumber: this field exists in the database but must NEVER be
    displayed in full in any report or export.
  - For analytics purposes, use only cc.cardtype (Vista, SuperiorCard, ColonialVoice,
    Distinguish) as a dimension.
  - PCI-DSS compliance: card numbers must be masked if ever displayed (show last 4 only).


================================================================================
3. SALES TERRITORY GOVERNANCE
================================================================================

3.1 Territory Assignment Authority
  - The territory on salesorderheader.territoryid is the authoritative territory for
    that order, regardless of the salesperson's current territory assignment.
  - A salesperson's current territory is in sales.salesperson.territoryid.
  - Territory changes are logged in sales.salesterritoryhistory with startdate/enddate.

3.2 Territory Hierarchy
  - Level 1 (Region Group): sales.salesterritory.group — North America, Europe, Pacific.
  - Level 2 (Territory): sales.salesterritory.name — e.g., Northwest, Canada, Germany.
  - Level 3 (Country): sales.salesterritory.countryregioncode — e.g., US, CA, DE, AU.

  Executive dashboards must default to Level 1 (Region Group) aggregation.
  Drill-down to Level 2 and Level 3 is permitted for authorized users.

3.3 Territory Performance Metrics
  Each territory tracks: salesytd, saleslastyear, costytd, costlastyear in sales.salesterritory.
  These are running totals updated by the system. For historical analysis, compute from
  salesorderheader/salesorderdetail directly.

3.4 Cross-Territory Attribution
  When a salesperson transfers territories mid-period:
  - Historical orders retain their original territoryid.
  - Performance reports for "Territory X" include all orders with territoryid = X,
    regardless of which salesperson handled them.
  - Performance reports for "Salesperson Y" include all orders where salespersonid = Y,
    regardless of territory.

3.5 Territory-Based Access Control
  Sales managers may view detailed data only for their assigned territory.
  Cross-territory data requires VP-level or executive access.
  Exception: Aggregate (territory-level totals) views are available to all sales managers
  for competitive benchmarking purposes.

4. PURCHASING & VENDOR COMPLIANCE
================================================================================

4.1 Vendor Classification
  Vendors are classified by their creditrating in purchasing.vendor:
  - 1 = Superior: No restrictions, preferred for strategic sourcing.
  - 2 = Excellent: Standard procurement process.
  - 3 = Above Average: Standard procurement process.
  - 4 = Average: Requires finance department approval for POs exceeding $10,000.
  - 5 = Below Average: Requires VP approval for any new PO. Subject to quarterly review.

4.2 Preferred Vendor Policy
  - Vendors with preferredvendorstatus = true must be prioritized in sourcing decisions.
  - When multiple vendors supply the same product (purchasing.productvendor), the preferred
    vendor must be selected unless their price exceeds the non-preferred vendor by more than 15%.
  - Preferred vendor status is reviewed annually based on delivery performance, quality, and pricing.

4.3 Inactive Vendor Policy
  - Vendors with activeflag = false in purchasing.vendor must NOT receive new purchase orders.
  - Existing open POs with inactive vendors must be reviewed within 30 days and either
    completed or transferred to an active vendor.
  - Reactivation requires quality audit and finance approval.

4.4 Quality Thresholds
  - Rejected quantity threshold: If SUM(rejectedqty) / SUM(receivedqty) exceeds 5% for
    any vendor in a rolling 12-month window, the vendor must be flagged for quality review.
  - Critical threshold: If the rejection rate exceeds 10%, the vendor must be suspended
    pending a formal quality audit.
  - Zero-tolerance items: Components used in safety-critical products (identified by
    productsubcategory = 'Brakes', 'Forks', 'Frames') have a 2% rejection threshold.

4.5 Purchase Price Controls
  - Standard price for each product-vendor combination is in purchasing.productvendor.standardprice.
  - Actual order price is in purchasing.purchaseorderdetail.unitprice.
  - Price variance (actual - standard) exceeding 10% requires procurement manager justification.
  - Price variance exceeding 20% requires VP approval.

4.6 Receiving Requirements
  - All purchase order details track: orderqty, receivedqty, rejectedqty.
  - Receiving ratio (receivedqty / orderqty) below 95% triggers a fulfillment investigation.
  - Short shipments must be documented and the vendor notified within 5 business days.

4.7 Freight and Shipping (Purchasing)
  - Shipping method for purchase orders: purchasing.purchaseorderheader.shipmethodid
    links to purchasing.shipmethod.
  - Freight costs: purchasing.purchaseorderheader.freight.
  - Ship methods have shipbase (fixed cost) and shiprate (per-unit cost).
  - Freight-to-subtotal ratio above 5% for any vendor triggers logistics review.

4.8 Single-Source Risk
  - Products supplied by only one vendor (single entry in purchasing.productvendor for
    a given productid) are flagged as single-source risk items.
  - Procurement must identify alternative vendors for all single-source products with
    annual spend exceeding $50,000.

5. HUMAN RESOURCES POLICIES
================================================================================

5.1 Compensation Confidentiality
  - Individual pay rates (humanresources.employeepayhistory.rate) are strictly confidential.
  - Reports must aggregate compensation data by department, job title, or pay frequency.
  - Minimum group size for any compensation report: 5 employees.
  - Pay rate combined with name or employee ID is NEVER permitted in any output.
  - Exception: HR department authorized users may access individual compensation for
    payroll processing and audit purposes only.

5.2 Pay Frequency Standards
  - humanresources.employeepayhistory.payfrequency values:
    1 = Monthly (salaried employees, typically salariedflag = true)
    2 = Biweekly (hourly employees, typically salariedflag = false)
  - When computing annual compensation:
    Monthly: rate * 12 (if monthly rate) or rate * 2080 (if hourly rate, 40hr/week)
    Biweekly: rate * 26 (if biweekly rate) or rate * 2080 (if hourly rate)
  - Always verify salariedflag to determine correct annualization.

5.3 Department Assignment Rules
  - Current department: the record in humanresources.employeedepartmenthistory where
    enddate IS NULL.
  - Historical department: records where enddate IS NOT NULL.
  - An employee may appear in multiple rows — each representing a department change.
  - Department transfer = new record with new departmentid, old record gets enddate set.
  - Departure = employee's currentflag becomes false in humanresources.employee.

5.4 Shift Assignment
  - Employees are assigned to shifts via humanresources.employeedepartmenthistory.shiftid.
  - Shift definitions in humanresources.shift: name, starttime, endtime.
  - Standard shifts: Day, Evening, Night.
  - Shift changes follow the same pattern as department changes in employeedepartmenthistory.

5.5 Organization Hierarchy
  - humanresources.employee.organizationnode defines the reporting structure.
  - The root node (shortest organizationnode) is the CEO.
  - Depth can be computed by counting separators in the organizationnode string.
  - Direct reports: employees whose organizationnode starts with a manager's node and
    has exactly one additional level.
  - Span of control: COUNT of direct reports per manager.

5.6 Leave Management
  - Vacation hours and sick leave hours are tracked in humanresources.employee.
  - Maximum vacation accrual: salaried employees accrue up to 80 hours/year,
    hourly employees accrue up to 40 hours/year.
  - Maximum sick leave accrual: 60 hours/year for all employees.
  - Departments with average vacation balance above 60 hours should encourage time-off usage.
  - Departments with average sick leave above 40 hours warrant wellness program review.

5.7 Hiring and Onboarding
  - New hire date is humanresources.employee.hiredate.
  - New hires should appear in their department within 5 business days (employeedepartmenthistory).
  - Probation period: first 90 days from hiredate.
  - Reports on "new hires" should filter by hiredate within the specified period.

5.8 Employee Demographics Reporting
  - Gender (humanresources.employee.gender): report as M/F counts or percentages.
  - Marital status (maritalstatus): S = Single, M = Married.
  - Birth date: use for age-range analysis only. Never report individual birth dates.
    Age ranges: Under 25, 25-34, 35-44, 45-54, 55-64, 65+.
  - All demographic breakdowns must respect the minimum group size of 5.

5.9 Job Candidate Data
  - humanresources.jobcandidate tracks applicant information.
  - Resume data is stored as XML in the resume column.
  - Parsed views: humanresources.vjobcandidate, vjobcandidateeducation, vjobcandidateemployment.
  - Candidate data is subject to data retention policies — records older than 2 years
    from application date should be anonymized unless the candidate was hired.

6. PRODUCT & INVENTORY MANAGEMENT STANDARDS
================================================================================

6.1 Product Lifecycle States
  A product's lifecycle state is determined by these fields in production.product:
  - Active for sale: sellstartdate <= data_date AND (sellenddate IS NULL OR sellenddate > data_date)
    AND discontinueddate IS NULL
  - End of sale: sellenddate IS NOT NULL AND sellenddate <= data_date AND discontinueddate IS NULL
  - Discontinued: discontinueddate IS NOT NULL
  - Not yet released: sellstartdate > data_date

  "Current product" reports must only include Active for sale products.
  "Full catalog" reports may include all states with a status column.

6.2 Product Classification Hierarchy
  - Level 1: production.productcategory.name — Bikes, Components, Clothing, Accessories
  - Level 2: production.productsubcategory.name — e.g., Mountain Bikes, Road Bikes, Jerseys
  - Level 3: production.product.name — individual product
  Joins: product.productsubcategoryid -> productsubcategory.productsubcategoryid
         productsubcategory.productcategoryid -> productcategory.productcategoryid
  Note: Some products have productsubcategoryid IS NULL — these are unclassified.

6.3 Product Line and Style
  production.product additional dimensions:
  - productline: R = Road, M = Mountain, T = Touring, S = Standard
  - class: H = High, M = Medium, L = Low
  - style: W = Women's, M = Men's, U = Universal
  These may be NULL for components and accessories.

6.4 Make vs Buy Classification
  - production.product.makeflag:
    true = Manufactured in-house (has work orders in production.workorder)
    false = Purchased from external vendor (has entries in purchasing.productvendor)
  - Cost analysis must segment by this flag.
  - Manufactured products use standardcost from production.product (or productcosthistory
    for time-accurate costs).
  - Purchased products cost = purchasing.productvendor.standardprice (or actual PO price).

6.5 Inventory Control Thresholds
  For each product in production.product:
  - safetystocklevel: minimum inventory to maintain. Below this triggers an ALERT.
  - reorderpoint: inventory level at which a new purchase order should be placed.
  Both are compared against SUM(quantity) from production.productinventory grouped by productid.

6.6 Inventory Location Management
  - production.productinventory tracks stock by: productid, locationid, shelf, bin.
  - production.location defines manufacturing/storage locations with costrate and availability.
  - Shelf = alphanumeric shelf identifier, Bin = numeric bin within shelf.
  - Total inventory for a product = SUM(quantity) across all locations.

6.7 Bill of Materials (BOM)
  - production.billofmaterials defines the component structure for assembled products.
  - productassemblyid = the parent/assembled product (NULL for top-level purchased components)
  - componentid = the child/part product
  - bomlevel = depth in the assembly tree (0 = top, 1 = first-level component, etc.)
  - startdate/enddate = effective dates for the BOM relationship
  - Current BOM: enddate IS NULL
  - unitmeasurecode = unit of measure for the component, perassemblyqty = quantity needed.

6.8 Product Photos and Documentation
  - Product photos: production.productphoto linked via production.productproductphoto.
  - Primary photo: productproductphoto.primary = true.
  - Product models: production.productmodel with catalog descriptions (XML) and instructions (XML).
  - Descriptions by culture/language: production.productmodelproductdescriptionculture
    links productmodel, productdescription, and culture.
  - Products without photos or descriptions should be flagged for marketing content completion.

6.9 Scrap and Waste Management
  - Scrap reasons: production.scrapreason (scrapreasonid, name).
  - Work order scrap: production.workorder.scrappedqty with scrapreasonid.
  - Scrap rate threshold: > 3% by product triggers manufacturing process review.
  - Scrap rate threshold: > 5% by scrap reason triggers root cause analysis.
  - Monthly scrap reports must include: product, scrap reason, scrapped quantity,
    scrap rate, and estimated cost impact (scrappedqty * standardcost).

6.10 Unit of Measure Standards
  - production.unitmeasure: unitmeasurecode (e.g., EA = Each, LB = Pound, IN = Inch).
  - Products have sizeunitmeasurecode and weightunitmeasurecode.
  - BOM components use unitmeasurecode for assembly quantities.
  - Reports must always display the unit of measure alongside any size/weight values.

7. DATA RETENTION, ARCHIVAL & INTEGRITY
================================================================================

7.1 Transaction Data Retention
  - Active transactions: production.transactionhistory
  - Archived transactions: production.transactionhistoryarchive
  - Retention rule: Transactions older than 5 years from the latest transaction date
    are moved to the archive table.
  - Active analysis should query production.transactionhistory.
  - Full historical analysis must UNION both tables:
    SELECT * FROM production.transactionhistory
    UNION ALL
    SELECT * FROM production.transactionhistoryarchive

7.2 Transaction Types
  Both transactionhistory and transactionhistoryarchive use:
  - transactiontype: W = Work Order, S = Sales Order, P = Purchase Order
  - referenceorderid: links to the originating order
  - referenceorderlineid: links to the specific line item

7.3 Archive Modification Policy
  - Archived records must NOT be modified or deleted without DBA approval.
  - Archive table indexes mirror active table indexes for query consistency.
  - Any reports comparing archived vs active data must document the data source.

7.4 Data Integrity Rules
  - All monetary fields must be non-negative (unitprice >= 0, orderqty >= 0, rate >= 0).
  - Dates must be logically consistent: startdate <= enddate, hiredate <= termination date,
    orderdate <= duedate <= shipdate (for normal flow).
  - Foreign key relationships must be respected — never assume an ID exists without verification.
  - rowguid fields are system-generated unique identifiers — never modify or use for joins
    (use the business key columns instead).

7.5 modifieddate Convention
  Nearly every table has a modifieddate column. This tracks the last modification timestamp
  for the row. It should NOT be used as a business event date. Always use the domain-specific
  date column (orderdate, hiredate, ratechangedate, etc.) for business analysis.

7.6 Audit Trail
  Changes to sensitive data (employee records, vendor status, product pricing) should be
  traceable through history tables:
  - Employee department changes: humanresources.employeedepartmenthistory
  - Employee pay changes: humanresources.employeepayhistory
  - Product cost changes: production.productcosthistory
  - Product price changes: production.productlistpricehistory
  - Territory reassignments: sales.salesterritoryhistory
  - Salesperson quota changes: sales.salespersonquotahistory


================================================================================
8. REPORT DISTRIBUTION & ACCESS CONTROL
================================================================================

8.1 Report Classification Levels
  - PUBLIC: Aggregated company-level metrics. May be shared externally.
    No PII, no individual records. Territory-group level or higher aggregation.
  - INTERNAL: Department and territory-level details. Available to all employees.
    May include territory names, department names, product details.
  - RESTRICTED: Individual salesperson performance, customer details, vendor terms.
    Available only to direct managers and authorized stakeholders.
  - CONFIDENTIAL: Compensation data, employee demographics, credit card details.
    Available only to HR, Finance, and executive leadership.

8.2 Executive Dashboard Standards
  Executive dashboards must show:
  - Territory-group level aggregates (North America, Europe, Pacific)
  - No individual customer, employee, or salesperson data
  - Fiscal year and quarter context
  - Trend indicators (MoM, QoQ, YoY)
  - Benchmark comparisons against targets

8.3 Department Reports
  - Department heads may view aggregated metrics for their department only.
  - Cross-department comparisons require VP-level access.
  - Employee-level data within a department requires HR authorization.

8.4 Sales Reports
  - Individual salesperson performance is visible only to:
    a) The salesperson themselves
    b) Their direct manager (via organizationnode hierarchy)
    c) VP of Sales and above
  - Territory-level summaries are available to all sales managers.
  - Customer-level detail (by name) is available to the assigned salesperson and their manager.

8.5 External Report Requirements
  Reports shared with external parties (investors, auditors, regulators) must:
  - Exclude ALL PII
  - Use only aggregated, anonymized data
  - Include data source and period disclaimers
  - Be reviewed by Finance before distribution
  - Follow the fiscal year calendar (Section 2.3)

8.6 Automated Report Schedules
  - Daily: Inventory alerts (below safety stock, below reorder point)
  - Weekly: Sales pipeline (in-process orders), vendor quality summary
  - Monthly: Full financial summary, territory performance, HR headcount
  - Quarterly: Executive KPI dashboard, vendor compliance review, product lifecycle review
  - Annually: Full year financial statements, workforce demographics, vendor re-evaluation

9. CUSTOMER DATA GOVERNANCE
================================================================================

9.1 Customer Types
  - Individual customers: sales.customer.personid IS NOT NULL, storeid IS NULL
  - Store/reseller customers: sales.customer.storeid IS NOT NULL
  - Some customers may have both personid and storeid (contact at a store).
  - Person type codes in person.person.persontype:
    SC = Store Contact, IN = Individual (Retail) Customer,
    SP = Sales Person, EM = Employee, VC = Vendor Contact, GC = General Contact

9.2 Customer Communication Preferences
  - person.person.emailpromotion indicates opt-in level:
    0 = No email promotions
    1 = From AdventureWorks only
    2 = From AdventureWorks and selected partners
  - Marketing campaigns must respect these preferences.
  - Bulk email lists must filter by emailpromotion >= 1.

9.3 Customer Address Usage
  - Addresses are linked via person.businessentityaddress with an addresstypeid.
  - Address types from person.addresstype: Billing, Home, Main Office, Primary, Shipping, Archive.
  - Shipping analysis should use addresstypeid for "Shipping" addresses.
  - Billing analysis should use addresstypeid for "Billing" addresses.
  - salesorderheader.billtoaddressid and shiptoaddressid directly reference person.address.

9.4 Customer Demographics
  - Individual customer demographics are stored as XML in person.person.demographics.
  - Pre-parsed view: sales.vpersondemographics provides:
    totalpurchaseytd, datefirstpurchase, birthdate, maritalstatus, yearlyincome,
    gender, totalchildren, numberchildrenathome, education, occupation,
    homeownerflag, numbercarsowned.
  - Store demographics are in sales.store.demographics (XML).
  - Pre-parsed view: sales.vstorewithdemographics provides:
    AnnualSales, AnnualRevenue, BankName, BusinessType, YearOpened, Specialty,
    SquareFeet, Brands, Internet, NumberEmployees.


================================================================================
10. DATA QUALITY STANDARDS
================================================================================

10.1 NULL Handling Rules
  - NULL in optional fields (middlename, addressline2, suffix) is expected and valid.
  - NULL in required fields (firstname, lastname, orderdate) indicates a data quality issue.
  - Reports should use COALESCE for optional display fields:
    COALESCE(middlename, '') or COALESCE(addressline2, 'N/A').
  - Aggregation functions (SUM, AVG, COUNT) automatically skip NULLs. Be aware that
    COUNT(*) includes NULL rows but COUNT(column) excludes them.

10.2 Duplicate Detection
  - Each entity has a rowguid column for system-level uniqueness.
  - Business-level duplicates should be detected by matching on business keys:
    Customers: personid + storeid combination
    Products: productnumber (unique)
    Vendors: accountnumber (unique)
    Employees: nationalidnumber (unique)

10.3 Referential Integrity
  - All foreign key columns should reference existing parent records.
  - When a query returns unexpected NULLs after a JOIN, check whether it should
    be an INNER JOIN (exclude orphans) or LEFT JOIN (include orphans as NULL).
  - salesorderheader.salespersonid may be NULL for online direct orders — this is valid.
  - salesorderheader.currencyrateid may be NULL for USD orders — this is valid.

10.4 Date Consistency
  Expected date ordering for sales: orderdate <= duedate, orderdate <= shipdate.
  Expected date ordering for work orders: startdate <= enddate <= duedate (ideally).
  Expected date ordering for employment: hiredate <= all employeedepartmenthistory.startdate.
  Any violations of these orderings should be flagged as data quality issues, not silently ignored.

10.5 View Usage
  The database contains pre-built views (prefixed with 'v'):
  - humanresources.vemployee, vemployeedepartment, vemployeedepartmenthistory
  - humanresources.vjobcandidate, vjobcandidateeducation, vjobcandidateemployment
  - person.vadditionalcontactinfo
  - production.vproductmodelcatalogdescription, vproductmodelinstructions
  - purchasing.vvendorwithaddresses, vvendorwithcontacts
  - sales.vindividualcustomer, vpersondemographics, vsalesperson
  - sales.vsalespersonsalesbyfiscalyears, vsalespersonsalesbyfiscalyearsdata
  - sales.vstorewithaddresses, vstorewithcontacts, vstorewithdemographics

  Views are convenient for quick lookups but may have performance implications for
  large datasets. For complex analytical queries, prefer joining base tables directly.
  Views should NEVER be used as the source for other views or materialized reports
  without DBA review.
