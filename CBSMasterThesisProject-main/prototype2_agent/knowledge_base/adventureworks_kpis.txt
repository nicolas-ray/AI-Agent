AdventureWorks Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
=================================================

This document defines every standard KPI used across AdventureWorks divisions.
Each KPI includes its business definition, the exact database tables and columns
required for computation, the SQL calculation logic, recommended time granularity,
and benchmark thresholds where applicable. These definitions serve as the single
source of truth for all dashboards, ad-hoc analyses, and executive reporting.


================================================================================
1. REVENUE & SALES KPIs
================================================================================

1.1 Total Revenue (Net Sales)
  Definition: The sum of all sales order line totals after discounts.
  Formula: SUM(unitprice * orderqty * (1 - unitpricediscount))
  Source tables: sales.salesorderdetail (sod), sales.salesorderheader (soh)
  Join condition: sod.salesorderid = soh.salesorderid
  Filter: Only orders where soh.status = 5 (shipped) should be counted in
    finalized revenue reports. Orders with status < 5 are in-progress.
  Granularity: Daily, monthly, quarterly, yearly.
  Note: There is NO pre-calculated linetotal or revenue column in salesorderdetail.
    Always derive it from unitprice * orderqty * (1 - unitpricediscount).

1.2 Gross Revenue (Before Discounts)
  Definition: Total revenue before any discounts are applied.
  Formula: SUM(unitprice * orderqty)
  Source tables: sales.salesorderdetail
  Use case: Comparing gross vs net revenue shows the impact of discounting strategy.

1.3 Total Discount Given
  Definition: The monetary value of all discounts applied across orders.
  Formula: SUM(unitprice * orderqty * unitpricediscount)
  Source tables: sales.salesorderdetail
  Benchmark: Discount as a percentage of gross revenue should stay below 8%.
    Exceeding 10% triggers a pricing review.

1.4 Discount Rate
  Definition: The average discount percentage applied across all line items.
  Formula: AVG(unitpricediscount) or SUM(discount_amount) / SUM(gross_revenue)
  Source tables: sales.salesorderdetail
  Granularity: Monthly, by territory, by product category.

1.5 Revenue Growth Rate
  Definition: Percentage change in total revenue compared to the prior period.
  Formula: (current_period_revenue - prior_period_revenue) / prior_period_revenue * 100
  Source tables: sales.salesorderdetail, sales.salesorderheader
  Time anchor: Always anchor to MAX(orderdate) in the data, not CURRENT_DATE,
    since the database contains historical data (2011-2014).
  Granularity: Month-over-month (MoM), quarter-over-quarter (QoQ), year-over-year (YoY).
  Benchmark: Healthy YoY growth target is 10-15%.

1.6 Average Order Value (AOV)
  Definition: Average revenue per sales order.
  Formula: Total Revenue / COUNT(DISTINCT salesorderid)
  Source tables: sales.salesorderdetail, sales.salesorderheader
  Granularity: Monthly, by channel (online vs offline), by territory.
  Benchmark: AOV below $500 may indicate excessive small-order volume.

1.7 Average Line Items per Order
  Definition: Average number of distinct products per order.
  Formula: COUNT(salesorderdetailid) / COUNT(DISTINCT salesorderid)
  Source tables: sales.salesorderdetail
  Use case: Cross-selling effectiveness measurement.

1.8 Sales by Territory
  Definition: Revenue broken down by sales territory.
  Source tables: sales.salesorderheader (soh), sales.salesterritory (st)
  Join: soh.territoryid = st.territoryid
  Dimensions: st.name (territory name), st.countryregioncode, st.group (region group).
  Territory groups: North America, Europe, Pacific.

1.9 Online vs Offline Revenue Split
  Definition: Revenue segmented by sales channel.
  Source tables: sales.salesorderheader, sales.salesorderdetail
  Dimension: salesorderheader.onlineorderflag (true = online, false = reseller/store).
  Benchmark: Online channel should represent at least 30% of total revenue.

1.10 Revenue per Salesperson
  Definition: Total revenue attributed to each salesperson.
  Source tables: sales.salesorderheader (soh), sales.salesorderdetail (sod),
    sales.salesperson (sp), humanresources.employee (e), person.person (p)
  Joins: soh.salespersonid = sp.businessentityid,
    sp.businessentityid = e.businessentityid,
    e.businessentityid = p.businessentityid
  Note: Orders where salespersonid IS NULL are online direct orders with no rep.
  Granularity: Monthly, quarterly.

1.11 Salesperson Quota Attainment
  Definition: Actual sales as a percentage of assigned quota.
  Formula: salesperson.salesytd / salesperson.salesquota * 100
  Source tables: sales.salesperson
  Historical quotas: sales.salespersonquotahistory for period-by-period tracking.
  Benchmark: Target is 100% attainment. Below 80% triggers performance review.
    Above 120% triggers quota reassessment.

1.12 Sales by Special Offer / Promotion
  Definition: Revenue and order volume for each active promotion.
  Source tables: sales.salesorderdetail (sod), sales.specialofferproduct (sop),
    sales.specialoffer (so)
  Joins: sod.specialofferid = sop.specialofferid AND sod.productid = sop.productid,
    sop.specialofferid = so.specialofferid
  Dimensions: so.description, so.type, so.category
  Note: When the user asks for revenue "by category" without mentioning promotions,
    use product categories (production.productcategory), NOT special offer categories.

1.13 Revenue by Currency
  Definition: Revenue broken down by transaction currency.
  Source tables: sales.salesorderheader (soh), sales.currencyrate (cr), sales.currency (c)
  Joins: soh.currencyrateid = cr.currencyrateid, cr.tocurrencycode = c.currencycode
  Note: Orders with currencyrateid IS NULL are in USD (base currency).

1.14 Tax Revenue
  Definition: Total tax collected on sales orders.
  Formula: SUM(taxamt) from sales.salesorderheader
  Granularity: By territory, by period.
  Note: Tax and freight are NEVER included in "revenue" unless the report explicitly
    says "total due" (which maps to salesorderheader.totaldue = subtotal + taxamt + freight).

1.15 Freight Cost on Sales
  Definition: Total shipping/freight cost on sales orders.
  Formula: SUM(freight) from sales.salesorderheader
  Related KPI: Freight-to-revenue ratio = SUM(freight) / SUM(subtotal).
  Benchmark: Freight should stay below 3% of subtotal.

1.16 Sales by Shipping Method
  Definition: Order count and revenue by shipping method.
  Source tables: sales.salesorderheader (soh), purchasing.shipmethod (sm)
  Join: soh.shipmethodid = sm.shipmethodid
  Dimensions: sm.name, sm.shipbase, sm.shiprate.

1.17 Credit Card Usage Distribution
  Definition: Breakdown of payment methods used for orders.
  Source tables: sales.salesorderheader (soh), sales.creditcard (cc)
  Join: soh.creditcardid = cc.creditcardid
  Dimensions: cc.cardtype (Vista, SuperiorCard, ColonialVoice, Distinguish).
  Note: Orders where creditcardid IS NULL were paid by purchase order (reseller channel).

1.18 Sales Reason Analysis
  Definition: Which reasons customers cite for purchasing.
  Source tables: sales.salesorderheadersalesreason (sohsr), sales.salesreason (sr)
  Join: sohsr.salesreasonid = sr.salesreasonid
  Dimensions: sr.name, sr.reasontype (Marketing, Promotion, Other).
  Use case: Marketing effectiveness measurement.

2. PRODUCT PERFORMANCE KPIs
================================================================================

2.1 Top Selling Products (by Volume)
  Definition: Products ranked by total units sold.
  Formula: SUM(orderqty) from sales.salesorderdetail, grouped by productid.
  Source tables: sales.salesorderdetail (sod), production.product (p)
  Join: sod.productid = p.productid

2.2 Top Selling Products (by Revenue)
  Definition: Products ranked by total revenue generated.
  Formula: SUM(unitprice * orderqty * (1 - unitpricediscount)) grouped by productid.
  Source tables: sales.salesorderdetail (sod), production.product (p)

2.3 Product Category Revenue
  Definition: Revenue grouped by product hierarchy.
  Source tables: sales.salesorderdetail (sod), production.product (p),
    production.productsubcategory (psc), production.productcategory (pc)
  Joins: sod.productid = p.productid,
    p.productsubcategoryid = psc.productsubcategoryid,
    psc.productcategoryid = pc.productcategoryid
  Categories: Bikes, Components, Clothing, Accessories.
  Note: Products with productsubcategoryid IS NULL are not assigned to any category.

2.4 Gross Margin per Product
  Definition: Profit margin based on list price vs standard cost.
  Formula: (listprice - standardcost) / listprice * 100
  Source table: production.product
  Note: Products with listprice = 0 are internal/non-sellable items — exclude from margin reports.
  Benchmark: Target gross margin is above 40%. Products below 20% margin need pricing review.
  SQL example (static margin from product table — no sales data needed):
    SELECT p.name,
           ROUND(((p.listprice - p.standardcost) / NULLIF(p.listprice, 0) * 100)::numeric, 2) AS gross_margin_pct
    FROM production.product p
    WHERE p.listprice > 0
    ORDER BY gross_margin_pct DESC
    LIMIT 10

2.4b Realised Profit Margin per Product (based on actual sales)
  Definition: Profit margin computed from actual sales revenue vs actual cost of goods sold.
  NEVER compute as revenue / gross_revenue — that is a discount ratio, not a margin.
  Formula: (actual_revenue - actual_cost) / actual_revenue * 100
  Where:
    actual_revenue = SUM(sod.unitprice * sod.orderqty * (1 - sod.unitpricediscount))
    actual_cost    = SUM(p.standardcost * sod.orderqty)
  Source tables: sales.salesorderdetail (sod), production.product (p)
  Join: sod.productid = p.productid
  SQL example:
    SELECT p.productid, p.name,
           ROUND((SUM(sod.unitprice * sod.orderqty * (1 - sod.unitpricediscount))
                  - SUM(p.standardcost * sod.orderqty))::numeric
                 / NULLIF(SUM(sod.unitprice * sod.orderqty * (1 - sod.unitpricediscount)), 0) * 100, 2)
           AS profit_margin_pct
    FROM sales.salesorderdetail sod
    JOIN production.product p ON sod.productid = p.productid
    GROUP BY p.productid, p.name
    ORDER BY profit_margin_pct DESC
    LIMIT 10

2.5 Product Cost History Trend
  Definition: How the standard manufacturing cost of a product has changed over time.
  Source table: production.productcosthistory
  Columns: productid, startdate, enddate, standardcost
  Use case: Identify cost inflation or efficiency improvements over production cycles.

2.6 Product List Price History Trend
  Definition: Historical changes in the customer-facing price.
  Source table: production.productlistpricehistory
  Columns: productid, startdate, enddate, listprice
  Use case: Pricing strategy analysis, correlation with sales volume changes.

2.7 Inventory Turnover
  Definition: How quickly inventory is sold relative to stock on hand.
  Formula: Total units sold (from salesorderdetail) / Average inventory (from productinventory)
  Source tables: sales.salesorderdetail, production.productinventory
  Benchmark: Turnover below 2x per year suggests overstocking. Above 12x may risk stockouts.

2.8 Current Inventory Levels
  Definition: Total stock on hand by product and location.
  Source table: production.productinventory
  Columns: productid, locationid, shelf, bin, quantity
  Join to production.location for location names (locationid -> location.locationid).

2.9 Inventory Below Safety Stock
  Definition: Products where current inventory falls below the safety threshold.
  Formula: WHERE pi.quantity < p.safetystocklevel
  Source tables: production.productinventory (pi), production.product (p)
  Join: pi.productid = p.productid
  Alert: These products should be flagged on inventory dashboards.

2.10 Inventory Below Reorder Point
  Definition: Products that need to be reordered.
  Formula: WHERE SUM(pi.quantity) < p.reorderpoint (aggregated across all locations)
  Source tables: production.productinventory (pi), production.product (p)
  Action: Generate purchase recommendation for the vendor supplying this product.

2.11 Product Scrap Rate
  Definition: Percentage of manufactured units scrapped during production.
  Formula: SUM(scrappedqty) / SUM(orderqty) * 100 from production.workorder
  Source tables: production.workorder (wo), production.scrapreason (sr)
  Join: wo.scrapreasonid = sr.scrapreasonid (for scrap reason breakdown)
  Benchmark: Scrap rate above 3% by product triggers manufacturing process review.

2.12 Days to Manufacture
  Definition: Average calendar days required to produce a product.
  Source table: production.product (daystomanufacture column)
  Alternative: Actual manufacturing time = AVG(enddate - startdate) from production.workorder
  Granularity: By product, by category.

2.13 Work Order Volume & Completion
  Definition: Number of work orders and their completion status.
  Source table: production.workorder
  Completed: enddate IS NOT NULL
  In progress: enddate IS NULL AND startdate IS NOT NULL
  Granularity: Monthly, by product.

2.14 Bill of Materials Depth
  Definition: Number of components required to assemble a product.
  Source table: production.billofmaterials
  Columns: productassemblyid (parent), componentid (child), bomlevel
  Use case: Supply chain complexity analysis, cost rollup.

2.15 Product Review Ratings
  Definition: Customer satisfaction based on product reviews.
  Source table: production.productreview
  Columns: productid, rating (1-5), reviewername, reviewdate, comments
  Formula: AVG(rating) grouped by productid
  Benchmark: Average rating below 3.0 requires product quality investigation.

2.16 Product Model & Description Coverage
  Definition: How many products have catalog descriptions and photos.
  Source tables: production.product (p), production.productmodel (pm),
    production.productmodelproductdescriptionculture (pmpdc),
    production.productphoto (pp), production.productproductphoto (ppp)
  Use case: Identify products missing marketing content.

2.17 Active vs Discontinued Products
  Definition: Current product catalog size vs historical.
  Source table: production.product
  Active: discontinueddate IS NULL AND sellstartdate <= current data date AND
    (sellenddate IS NULL OR sellenddate > current data date)
  Discontinued: discontinueddate IS NOT NULL

2.18 Product Line Distribution
  Definition: Revenue and product count by product line.
  Source table: production.product
  Dimension: productline column (R = Road, M = Mountain, T = Touring, S = Standard).
  Also: class (H = High, M = Medium, L = Low), style (W = Women, M = Men, U = Universal).

2.19 Make vs Buy Analysis
  Definition: Cost and volume comparison between manufactured and purchased products.
  Source table: production.product
  Dimension: makeflag (true = manufactured in-house, false = purchased from vendor).
  Metrics: Count, average cost, total revenue, margin comparison.

3. CUSTOMER KPIs
================================================================================

3.1 Total Customer Count
  Definition: Number of distinct customers in the system.
  Formula: COUNT(DISTINCT customerid) from sales.customer
  Segment by: personid IS NOT NULL (individual) vs storeid IS NOT NULL (store/reseller).

3.2 Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
  Definition: Total revenue generated by a customer across all their orders.
  Formula: SUM(unitprice * orderqty * (1 - unitpricediscount)) per customerid
  Source tables: sales.customer (c), sales.salesorderheader (soh), sales.salesorderdetail (sod)
  Joins: c.customerid = soh.customerid, soh.salesorderid = sod.salesorderid
  Granularity: Rank customers by CLV; segment into deciles.

3.3 Customer Acquisition by Period
  Definition: Number of new customers acquired in each time period.
  Formula: MIN(orderdate) per customerid from salesorderheader gives the acquisition date.
    Count distinct customerid grouped by EXTRACT(YEAR FROM min_orderdate), EXTRACT(MONTH FROM min_orderdate).
  Use case: Track growth of customer base over time.

3.4 Repeat Purchase Rate
  Definition: Percentage of customers who have placed more than one order.
  Formula: COUNT(customers with order_count > 1) / COUNT(all customers) * 100
  Source tables: sales.salesorderheader grouped by customerid
  Benchmark: Repeat rate above 30% is considered healthy for B2B/reseller channels.

3.5 Customer Revenue Concentration (Pareto Analysis)
  Definition: What percentage of revenue comes from the top N% of customers.
  Use case: If top 10% of customers generate more than 60% of revenue, the business
    faces concentration risk.
  Source tables: sales.salesorderheader, sales.salesorderdetail grouped by customerid.

3.6 Average Orders per Customer
  Definition: How many orders a customer places on average.
  Formula: COUNT(salesorderid) / COUNT(DISTINCT customerid)
  Source table: sales.salesorderheader
  Segment by: Individual vs store customers, online vs offline.

3.7 Customer Geographic Distribution
  Definition: Where customers are located geographically.
  Source tables: sales.customer (c), person.businessentityaddress (bea),
    person.address (a), person.stateprovince (sp), person.countryregion (cr)
  Joins: For individual customers, c.personid = bea.businessentityid.
    For store customers, c.storeid = bea.businessentityid.
    bea.addressid = a.addressid, a.stateprovinceid = sp.stateprovinceid,
    sp.countryregioncode = cr.countryregioncode
  Dimensions: city, stateprovince, countryregion.

3.8 Store (Reseller) Performance
  Definition: Revenue and order metrics for store/reseller customers.
  Source tables: sales.customer (c), sales.store (s)
  Join: c.storeid = s.businessentityid
  Columns: s.name (store name), s.salespersonid (assigned rep), s.demographics (XML data).
  Segment by: salesperson assignment, territory.

3.9 Individual Customer Demographics
  Definition: Demographic profile of individual (non-store) customers.
  Source tables: sales.customer (c), person.person (p)
  Join: c.personid = p.businessentityid
  Columns: p.persontype (IN = Individual, SC = Store Contact, SP = Sales Person, etc.)
  Note: Demographic XML in person.person.demographics can be parsed for income, education, etc.
    The view sales.vpersondemographics provides pre-parsed fields.

3.10 Shopping Cart Abandonment
  Definition: Items added to shopping carts but never converted to orders.
  Source table: sales.shoppingcartitem
  Analysis: Compare shoppingcartitem entries with salesorderdetail to find unconverted items.
  Columns: shoppingcartid, productid, quantity, datecreated.

4. PURCHASING & SUPPLY CHAIN KPIs
================================================================================

4.1 Total Purchase Spend
  Definition: Total value of all purchase orders.
  Formula: SUM(subtotal) from purchasing.purchaseorderheader
  Alternative detail level: SUM(unitprice * orderqty) from purchasing.purchaseorderdetail
  Granularity: Monthly, quarterly, yearly, by vendor.

4.2 Purchase Order Volume
  Definition: Number of purchase orders placed per period.
  Formula: COUNT(DISTINCT purchaseorderid) from purchasing.purchaseorderheader
  Segment by: vendor, employee (buyer), status.

4.3 Purchase Order Status Distribution
  Definition: Breakdown of POs by their current status.
  Source table: purchasing.purchaseorderheader
  Status values: 1 = Pending, 2 = Approved, 3 = Rejected, 4 = Complete.
  Use case: Monitor procurement pipeline health.

4.4 Vendor Lead Time
  Definition: Average time from order to delivery by vendor.
  Source table: purchasing.productvendor
  Column: averageleadtime (in days)
  Granularity: By vendor, by product.
  Benchmark: Lead time above 30 days for critical components requires alternative sourcing.

4.5 Vendor Rejected Quantity Rate
  Definition: Quality metric — percentage of received goods rejected.
  Formula: SUM(rejectedqty) / SUM(receivedqty) * 100 from purchasing.purchaseorderdetail
  Grouped by: vendorid (from purchaseorderheader)
  Benchmark: Rate above 5% in a rolling 12-month window triggers vendor quality review.

4.6 Top Vendors by Spend
  Definition: Vendors ranked by total purchasing spend.
  Source tables: purchasing.purchaseorderheader (poh), purchasing.vendor (v)
  Join: poh.vendorid = v.businessentityid
  Columns: v.name, v.accountnumber, v.creditrating

4.7 Vendor Credit Risk
  Definition: Vendors flagged by their credit rating.
  Source table: purchasing.vendor
  Column: creditrating (1 = Superior, 2 = Excellent, 3 = Above Average,
    4 = Average, 5 = Below Average)
  Policy: Vendors with creditrating >= 4 require finance approval for new POs.

4.8 Preferred Vendor Utilization
  Definition: What percentage of spend goes to preferred vendors.
  Source tables: purchasing.vendor (v), purchasing.purchaseorderheader (poh)
  Filter: v.preferredvendorstatus = true
  Benchmark: At least 70% of spend should go to preferred vendors.

4.9 Freight Cost Ratio (Purchasing)
  Definition: Shipping cost as a percentage of purchase subtotal.
  Formula: SUM(freight) / SUM(subtotal) * 100 from purchasing.purchaseorderheader
  Benchmark: Should stay below 5%.

4.10 Product-Vendor Coverage
  Definition: How many approved vendors supply each product.
  Source table: purchasing.productvendor
  Use case: Single-source products (only one vendor) represent supply chain risk.

4.11 Purchase Price Variance
  Definition: Difference between standard price and actual purchase price.
  Source tables: purchasing.productvendor (standardprice), purchasing.purchaseorderdetail (unitprice)
  Formula: AVG(pod.unitprice - pv.standardprice) per product per vendor
  Use case: Identify cost overruns and negotiate better terms.

4.12 Receiving Efficiency
  Definition: How closely received quantities match ordered quantities.
  Formula: SUM(receivedqty) / SUM(orderqty) * 100 from purchasing.purchaseorderdetail
  Benchmark: Below 95% indicates supplier fulfillment issues.

5. HUMAN RESOURCES KPIs
================================================================================

5.1 Total Headcount
  Definition: Number of active employees.
  Formula: COUNT(*) from humanresources.employee WHERE currentflag = true
  Granularity: By department, by job title, by gender, by shift.

5.2 Employee Turnover Rate
  Definition: Rate at which employees leave the organization.
  Formula: Employees with enddate in humanresources.employeedepartmenthistory in a period
    divided by average headcount in that period, multiplied by 100.
  Note: An enddate in employeedepartmenthistory could mean a transfer (new record with
    same businessentityid, different department) or a departure (no subsequent record).
    True departures have currentflag = false in humanresources.employee.
  Benchmark: Annual turnover above 15% is a concern.

5.3 Average Employee Tenure
  Definition: How long employees have been with the company.
  Formula: AVG(latest_data_date - hiredate) for active employees (currentflag = true)
  Source table: humanresources.employee
  Note: Use MAX(orderdate) from salesorderheader as the reference date, not CURRENT_DATE.

5.4 Department Headcount
  Definition: Employee count per department.
  Source tables: humanresources.employee (e), humanresources.employeedepartmenthistory (edh),
    humanresources.department (d)
  Joins: e.businessentityid = edh.businessentityid, edh.departmentid = d.departmentid
  Filter: edh.enddate IS NULL (current department assignment) AND e.currentflag = true

5.5 Department Group Distribution
  Definition: Employee count grouped by department group.
  Source table: humanresources.department
  Column: groupname (Executive General and Administration, Inventory Management,
    Manufacturing, Quality Assurance, Research and Development, Sales and Marketing).

5.6 Pay Rate Analysis
  Definition: Compensation metrics by department or job title.
  Source tables: humanresources.employeepayhistory (eph), humanresources.employee (e),
    humanresources.employeedepartmenthistory (edh), humanresources.department (d)
  Current rate: The record in employeepayhistory with the most recent ratechangedate per employee.
  Metrics: AVG(rate), MIN(rate), MAX(rate), PERCENTILE_CONT(0.5) WITHIN GROUP (ORDER BY rate) as median
  Column: payfrequency (1 = monthly, 2 = biweekly)
  Policy: NEVER show individual salaries — always aggregate by group of at least 5.

5.7 Pay Rate Trend
  Definition: How compensation has changed over time.
  Source table: humanresources.employeepayhistory
  Columns: businessentityid, ratechangedate, rate
  Use case: Identify pay raises, compression issues, cost trends.

5.8 Shift Distribution
  Definition: Employee count per shift.
  Source tables: humanresources.employeedepartmenthistory (edh), humanresources.shift (s)
  Join: edh.shiftid = s.shiftid
  Filter: edh.enddate IS NULL (current assignment)
  Shifts: Day (starttime/endtime), Evening, Night.

5.9 Vacation and Sick Leave Utilization
  Definition: Average leave hours by department or job title.
  Source table: humanresources.employee
  Columns: vacationhours, sickleavehours
  Benchmark: Average vacation usage below 40 hours/year may indicate employee burnout risk.
    Sick leave above 60 hours/year per department warrants investigation.

5.10 Salaried vs Hourly Workforce Mix
  Definition: Breakdown of employees by compensation type.
  Source table: humanresources.employee
  Column: salariedflag (true = salaried, false = hourly)
  Granularity: By department, by job title.

5.11 Gender Distribution
  Definition: Workforce gender composition.
  Source table: humanresources.employee
  Column: gender (M, F)
  Policy: Always report as aggregated counts or percentages, never individual records.
    Minimum group size of 5 for any demographic breakdown.

5.12 Hiring Trend
  Definition: Number of new hires per period.
  Source table: humanresources.employee
  Column: hiredate
  Formula: COUNT(*) grouped by EXTRACT(YEAR FROM hiredate), EXTRACT(MONTH FROM hiredate)

5.13 Job Title Distribution
  Definition: Employee count by job title.
  Source table: humanresources.employee
  Column: jobtitle
  Use case: Organizational structure analysis, span-of-control metrics.

5.14 Organization Hierarchy Depth
  Definition: Management layers in the organization.
  Source table: humanresources.employee
  Column: organizationnode (hierarchyid-like path string)
  Use case: Org chart analysis, reporting line depth.

6. OPERATIONAL EFFICIENCY KPIs
================================================================================

6.1 Order Fulfillment Time
  Definition: Average calendar days from order placement to shipment.
  Formula: AVG(shipdate - orderdate) from sales.salesorderheader
  Filter: Only shipped orders (shipdate IS NOT NULL)
  Benchmark: Target is under 7 days. Above 14 days is critical.
  Granularity: By territory, by shipping method, by period.

6.2 On-Time Shipping Rate
  Definition: Percentage of orders shipped on or before the due date.
  Formula: COUNT(WHERE shipdate <= duedate) / COUNT(shipped orders) * 100
  Source table: sales.salesorderheader
  Benchmark: Target is 95%+. Below 90% triggers logistics review.

6.3 Late Shipment Rate
  Definition: Percentage of orders shipped after the due date.
  Formula: COUNT(WHERE shipdate > duedate) / COUNT(shipped orders) * 100
  Source table: sales.salesorderheader

6.4 Order Revision Frequency
  Definition: How often orders are revised after initial placement.
  Source table: sales.salesorderheader
  Column: revisionnumber
  Benchmark: Orders with revisionnumber > 2 indicate process issues.

6.5 Work Order Completion Rate
  Definition: Percentage of manufacturing work orders completed on schedule.
  Formula: COUNT(WHERE enddate IS NOT NULL) / COUNT(*) * 100
  Source table: production.workorder
  Granularity: Monthly, by product.

6.6 Work Order Cycle Time
  Definition: Average time to complete a manufacturing work order.
  Formula: AVG(enddate - startdate) from production.workorder WHERE enddate IS NOT NULL
  Benchmark: Compare to production.product.daystomanufacture for planned vs actual.

6.7 Work Order Routing Efficiency
  Definition: Actual vs planned costs and hours in manufacturing operations.
  Source table: production.workorderrouting
  Formulas:
    Cost variance = SUM(actualcost - plannedcost)
    Hours efficiency = SUM(actualresourcehrs) / expected hours by location
  Granularity: By locationid (join to production.location for names), by product.

6.8 Manufacturing Location Utilization
  Definition: How busy each production location is.
  Source tables: production.workorderrouting (wor), production.location (l)
  Join: wor.locationid = l.locationid
  Metrics: Total actual hours, total work orders, avg cost per work order.
  Columns from location: name, costrate, availability.

6.9 Tax Collection Rate
  Definition: Effective tax rate on sales.
  Formula: SUM(taxamt) / SUM(subtotal) * 100 from sales.salesorderheader
  Granularity: By territory, by state/province.
  Use case: Verify tax compliance across jurisdictions.

6.10 Transaction Volume
  Definition: Number of inventory transactions per period.
  Source table: production.transactionhistory
  Columns: transactionid, productid, transactiondate, transactiontype (W = Work Order,
    S = Sales Order, P = Purchase Order), quantity, actualcost
  Note: production.transactionhistoryarchive contains older records.
    For full historical analysis, UNION both tables.

6.11 Document Management
  Definition: Internal document tracking metrics.
  Source table: production.document
  Columns: title, owner, status (1 = Pending, 2 = Approved, 3 = Obsolete),
    folderflag, changenumber
  Use case: Track documentation completeness and review cadence.


================================================================================
7. FINANCIAL KPIs
================================================================================

7.1 Total Due (Gross Order Value)
  Definition: Total amount owed by customers including tax and freight.
  Formula: SUM(totaldue) from sales.salesorderheader
  Note: totaldue = subtotal + taxamt + freight. This is the cash collection target.

7.2 Subtotal (Pre-Tax Revenue)
  Definition: Total order value before tax and freight.
  Formula: SUM(subtotal) from sales.salesorderheader
  Note: subtotal is the header-level pre-tax total. For line-level detail, use
    SUM(unitprice * orderqty * (1 - unitpricediscount)) from salesorderdetail.

7.3 Effective Tax Rate by Region
  Formula: SUM(taxamt) / SUM(subtotal) * 100
  Source table: sales.salesorderheader
  Grouped by: territoryid -> salesterritory
  Cross-reference: sales.salestaxrate for statutory rates by stateprovinceid.

7.4 Currency Exposure
  Definition: Revenue breakdown by transaction currency.
  Source tables: sales.salesorderheader (soh), sales.currencyrate (cr)
  Join: soh.currencyrateid = cr.currencyrateid
  Dimensions: cr.fromcurrencycode, cr.tocurrencycode, cr.averagerate, cr.endofdayrate
  Risk: Large exposure to a single non-USD currency is a foreign exchange risk.

7.5 Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
  Definition: Total standard cost of products sold.
  Formula: SUM(p.standardcost * sod.orderqty) from sales.salesorderdetail sod
    JOIN production.product p ON sod.productid = p.productid
  Alternative: Use productcosthistory for time-accurate costs.

7.6 Gross Profit
  Definition: Revenue minus cost of goods sold.
  Formula: Total Revenue (KPI 1.1) - COGS (KPI 7.5)

7.7 Gross Profit Margin
  Formula: Gross Profit / Total Revenue * 100
  Benchmark: Company-wide target is 45%+.


================================================================================
8. CALCULATION NOTES & CONVENTIONS
================================================================================

8.1 Date Anchoring
  The AdventureWorks database contains historical data spanning 2011-2014.
  NEVER use CURRENT_DATE or NOW() as a time anchor — queries will return zero rows.
  Always anchor to: (SELECT MAX(orderdate) FROM sales.salesorderheader)

8.2 Fiscal Year
  AdventureWorks fiscal year runs July 1 to June 30.
  Q1 = Jul-Sep, Q2 = Oct-Dec, Q3 = Jan-Mar, Q4 = Apr-Jun.
  To compute fiscal year: CASE WHEN EXTRACT(MONTH FROM date) >= 7
    THEN EXTRACT(YEAR FROM date) + 1 ELSE EXTRACT(YEAR FROM date) END

8.3 Currency
  Base currency is USD. Non-USD transactions have currencyrateid populated on
  salesorderheader, which links to sales.currencyrate for conversion rates.
  The sales.currency table has the full currency list.

8.4 Revenue Formula (Canonical)
  unitprice * orderqty * (1 - unitpricediscount)
  Always derive from salesorderdetail. Never assume a pre-calculated column exists.

8.5 Active vs Inactive Entities
  Active employees: humanresources.employee.currentflag = true
  Active vendors: purchasing.vendor.activeflag = true
  Active products: production.product.discontinueddate IS NULL
  Current department: humanresources.employeedepartmenthistory.enddate IS NULL

8.6 Primary Key Conventions
  businessentityid is the universal identifier for people, stores, vendors, and employees.
  It comes from person.businessentity and links to person.person, humanresources.employee,
  purchasing.vendor, and sales.store.
  customerid (from sales.customer) is separate — it links to personid or storeid.

8.7 Rounding
  All monetary values in final reports should be rounded to 2 decimal places.
  Use ROUND(value, 2) in SQL.

8.8 INTERVAL Syntax
  PostgreSQL requires: INTERVAL '12 months' (number and unit inside ONE quoted string).
  Never write: INTERVAL '12' MONTHS (this is invalid).
